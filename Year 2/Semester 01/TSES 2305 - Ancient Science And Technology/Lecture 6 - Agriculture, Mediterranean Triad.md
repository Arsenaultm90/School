
# The Mediterranean Triad (Core Framework)

#### The Triad
- **Grain**
- **Wine**
- **Oil** (olive oil – implied, though not detailed in this section)

> The Mediterranean diet and economy were structured around **grain for calories**, **wine for nutrition and water safety**, and **oil for fat, fuel, and ritual**.

This section focuses mainly on **grain**.



---
# Grain Storage and State Control

#### Evidence from Egypt
- **Old Kingdom granaries (4th Dynasty, 2613–2498 BC)**
    - Circular arrangement
    - Multiple silos
    - Domed tops
- Indicates:
    - Centralized collection
    - State-managed storage
    - Redistribution economy

> Grain storage = political power + food security



---
# Wheat (Primary Bread Grain)

#### Type
- **Durum wheat** (“hard wheat”)

#### Digestion & Processing
- Hard wheat is **not efficiently absorbed raw**
- **Milling**:
    - Breaks down grain
    - Increases nutritional value

#### Brewing vs. Baking
- Brewing:
    - Makes water safe
    - Breaks down wheat more completely
    - Allows higher calorie absorption than baking
- Standing water:
    - Causes dysentery
    - Link to mosquitoes/malaria unknown to ancients

#### Fermentation
- Beer and wine:
    - Disinfect water
    - Preserve calories
    - Improve nutrition

> **Beer was often more nutritious than bread**

(Comparandum: tea boiling in China, c. 220 BC)



---
# Bread Production & Cultural Importance

#### Rome: Tomb of Eurysaces
- Tomb shaped like a **bread oven**
- Shows pride in profession
- Relief scenes depict:
    - Milling grain
    - Kneading dough
    - Baking
    - Packaging bread
- Inscription dates to reign of **Tiberius**

#### Bread Types
- Early bread mostly **flatbread**
    - Pita (source of “pizza” and English _piece_)
- Yeast in antiquity:
    - Inefficient
    - Limited rise
- More like:
    - Flatbread
    - Focaccia

#### Cultural Expressions
- Latin proverb: _non satur sine pane_
    - “No meal is complete without bread”
- Vergil, _Aeneid_ 7.115:
    - “Eating their tables” = bread as plate (pizza)

# Barley (Earlier and More Versatile than Wheat)

#### Agricultural Advantages
- Earlier crop than wheat
- Cheaper
- Hardier
- High yield
- High germination rate

#### Disadvantages
- Higher spoilage rate than wheat

#### Economic & Social Use
- Evidence of **royal monopolies**
- Preferred grain for:
    - Beer
    - Animal feed (especially pigs)

#### Egypt
- Workers:
    - Ate **wheat**
    - Drank **barley beer**

#### Barley: Chronology and Geography
- First cultivated:
    - East Nile Delta → Crete → Near East & Europe
    - c. **9000 BC**
- Earliest remains:
    - Ohali (Sea of Galilee), c. **8500 BC**
- Ration tablets:
    - King Urukagina of Girsu (c. 2350 BC)
        - 20 measures/month (adults)
        - 10 measures/month (children)
- Also appears on:
    - Knossos (Crete)
    - Pylos (southern Greece)


#### Barley, Beer, and Religion
**Beer Production**
- Barley brewed into beer for:
    - Workers at **Deir el-Medina**
    - Builders of Akhetaton (c. 1350 BC)

#### Ritual Use
- Barley beer (**kykeon**) in rites of **Demeter at Eleusis**
- Evidence from:
    - Ninnion tablet
    - Coins
    - Comedies

# Sociology of Food

#### Roman Attitudes Toward Barley
- Fed to:
    - Pack animals
    - Defeated legions
    - Punished soldiers
- Barley = symbol of **lower status**
- Exception:
    - Religious rituals
    - Cult contexts (e.g. Demeter)

> Food choice reflects **status, discipline, and moral judgment**



---
# Rye (Secale)

#### Environmental Role
- Grows where wheat cannot:
    - Higher elevations
    - Colder climates
- Especially important on:
    - Northern borders of Roman Empire

#### Social Role
- Known to Greeks (Macedonia, Thrace, Thessaly)
- Used:
    - By the poor
    - In bad harvest years
    - Mixed with wheat as extender
