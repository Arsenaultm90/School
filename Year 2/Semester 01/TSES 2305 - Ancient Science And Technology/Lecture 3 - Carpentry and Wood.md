# Animal-Based Glues in Antiquity

#### Materials Used
- **Skin**
- **Bones**
- **Tendons**
- Common animal sources:
    - Horse
    - Rabbit
    - Fish (especially important as **binding agents in paint**)

#### Uses
- Furniture construction
- Caskets
- Wood veneer
- Gluing thin strips of wood together
- Repairing pottery (mentioned by **Pliny**)

#### Terminology
- Greek **kola** → English _collagen_
- Latin **gluten** → English _gluten_

#### Downsides of Animal Glue
- Must be applied **warm**
    - Becomes unworkable below **~30°C**
- Begins to **smell** over time
- **Loses effectiveness** with age
- Prone to **mold** (organic material)

# Prosthetics and Glue in Egypt

### Big Toe Prosthetics
- **Only the big toe** received prosthetics
    - Loss of other toes does **not** cause limping
- Prosthetics made of:
    - Leather
    - Wood
    - Bound together with glue

### Cartonnage Prosthetics
- **Cartonnage** = linen + glue + plaster
- Method:
    - Linen soaked in glue and plaster
    - Layered sheets
    - Formed to shape
    - Set under moderate pressure

# Drilling and Dowels

#### Bow Drill
- Used for:
    - Chair legs
    - Decorative wood details
- Motion:
    - Alternating **pull–push** motion
- Drill bit:
    - Spoon-shaped end
    - Cuts in **both directions**
- Term “boring” is literal due to repetitive motion

#### Dowels
- Used extensively in furniture construction
- **Coppice wood**:
    - Trees grown close together
    - Produces straight, thin trunks
- Advantages:
    - Natural dowels stronger than cut dowels
    - Preserves original fiber structure
    - Trunk fibers grow in alternating spirals → strength in bending

#### Structural Insight
- Cutting lumber disrupts natural tensile strength
- Therefore:
    - **Ship masts** often made from a single tree trunk
    - Used by Egyptians, Greeks, and Romans

# The Lathe

#### Definition
- A lathe turns an object on a **horizontal axis**
- Distinct from a potter’s wheel (vertical axis)

#### Identifying Lathe Work Archaeologically
- Striation (circular tool marks)
- Evidence of **mandrels** (attachments at top and bottom)
- Perfectly round diameters


---
# History of the Lathe

#### Origins
- Invented in **Egypt c. 1300 BC**
- Early lathes:
    - Two-person operation
    - Similar mechanics to potter’s wheel

#### Prestige Objects
- Mycenaean and Etruscan lathe-turned bowls
- Found in **elite tombs**
- Early lathe-turned wood objects = **status symbols**


---
# Pole Lathe (Egyptian Innovation)

#### Mechanics
- Operated by **one person**
- Components:
    - Vertical post
    - Flexible palm branch
    - Strap looped around object
    - Foot-controlled plank
- Cutting occurs on **downward stroke**
- Limitation:
    - Blade cuts only one way

#### Technique
- Exterior worked first (reduces weight)
- Interior hollowed while object still mounted
- Mandrels removed at very end
- Objects polished
- Stone sealed with **beeswax** (marble is porous)


---
# Dating Lathe-Turned Objects

#### Relative Dating Indicators
- More decorative complexity = later
- More complex royal naming = later
    - **Serekh** (earlier)
    - **Cartouche** (from 5th Dynasty onward)


---
# Roman Bow Lathe

#### Features
- In use by **at least 300 BC**
- Horizontal axis
- Adjustable length
- Likely two-person operation
- Faster rotation → finer striations

#### Use
- Wood
- Bone
- Stone
- Both prestige and everyday objects

#### Key Insight
> **New technologies do not replace old ones immediately**  
> (They coexist)

# Preservation of Ancient Wood

#### Rare Survival Contexts
- Egypt (dry climate)
- Shipwrecks (cold, deep water)
- Herculaneum:
    - Volcanic ash + light rain
    - Preserved carbonized wood
- Burned cities (very rare, minimal remains)



---
# Herculaneum: Structural Wood Evidence

#### Beams and Floors
- Visible:
    - Beam ends
    - Joists
    - Floor thickness preserved by ash
- Indicates:
    - Multi-story buildings
    - Heavy structural loads
- Roman law (AD 64):
    - Max **4 storeys**

#### Construction Techniques
- Tuscan style:
    - Beams extend beyond jambs
    - Reduces pressure on vertical supports
- Ceramic beam jackets:
    - Reduce pressure
    - Improve longevity

# Doors, Windows, and Partitions

#### Doors
- Wider openings → lintels extend farther
- Small doors → minimal lintel extension

#### Windows
- Ground floor windows had metal grilles
- Three casement types:
    - Timber-framed
    - Boxed windows
    - Extended lintel/threshold
- Shutters:
    - Multiple boards
    - Horizontal cross-pieces

#### Partitions
- Wood frames
- Non-load-bearing
- Often added later
