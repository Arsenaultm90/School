
This course is about designing games. The other courses in the stream will use design to improve your projects, but focus on the technical side of bringing your vision to life.


### What is a Game Designer
• Sets the experience goal  
• Design game rules to help achieve that goal  
• Evaluate if game meets goals  

Considers team capabilities, finances, deadlines, market  
shifts, and more.

1. Knows the questions to ask about the game  
2. Is able to prioritize those questions  
3. Is able to find answers to those questions  
4. Is able to evaluate how effective those answers are  
5. Is able to communicate the design decisions to the rest of the team


### Game Mechanics
**Mechanics are the rules and actions available to the player.**
- Explicit
- Designed
- Written down or coded

Examples:
- Jumping
- Shooting
- Turn-taking
- Resource costs
- Win/lose conditions
- Cooldowns

> Mechanics answer: **“What can the player do?”**


### Game Dynamics
**Dynamics are what happens when players interact with mechanics over time.**
- Emergent
- Not directly coded
- Often unpredictable

Examples:
- Risk-taking
- Camping
- Hoarding resources
- Bluffing
- Speedrunning
- Dominant strategies

> Dynamics answer: **“What actually happens when people play?”**


### How experiences emerge from rules
**Rules → Mechanics → Dynamics → Player Experience**
- You design **rules**
- Rules create **mechanics**
- Mechanics interact to form **dynamics**
- Dynamics produce **experience**

> **You never design the experience directly.**  
> You design the system that _produces_ it
