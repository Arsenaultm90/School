We have two main ways to give resources value:  
1. Scarcity: If it is hard to come by, it’s valuable.  
2. Utility: If it is useful to our goal, it’s valuable.  

Sometimes increasing scarcity can decrease utility.  
Increasing utility of one resource may devalue another.

#### Utility: Useful for our Goal.  
Value through Reward.

Reward of Access: Unlocked Game Content; Keys, unlocked doors  
Reward of Facility: Avatar Enhancement; Powers, stats  
Reward of Sustenance: Mitigating Burden; Food, ammo, healing

# Feedback Dynamics

In systems design, you need to consider how resources are affected over time and how this affects the player experience.  

When some resource affects how that resource is obtained in the future - or how player success affects future success - this is a feedback loop.


#### Positive Feedback
Why include positive feedback?  
	Helps end dominant games quickly  
	Explosive growth makes you feel awesome!  
	Positive feedback is addicting  

Where could it go wrong?  
	“Death spiral” for worse players  
	If too slow, can “guarantee” a loss eventually and you’re encourages to quit/lose  
	VERY explosive resource accumulation


#### Negative Feedback
Why include negative feedback?  
	Closes the gap between stronger and weaker players  
	Can lead to dramatic events  
	Can give you a fighting chance  

Where could it go wrong?  
	Can draw out a game  
	Makes experienced players feel artificially weakened  
	Enforces a specific way of playing


You can combine positive and negative feedback to keep players fighting, but ultimately lead to the game ending before it draws out.
