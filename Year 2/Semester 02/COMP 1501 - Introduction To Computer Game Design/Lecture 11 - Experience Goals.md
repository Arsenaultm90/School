
# Choosing Actions
Abstracting Intentions into Rules

We know mechanics as a general concept for individual rules in a game, but we can break this  
down into a more formal set. 

#### Mechanics
**Space:** 
	Alone, it’s purely mathematical. It can be continuous, or discrete.
**Time:** 
	Our use of time depends on what we want to accomplish. It can be continuous, or discrete.
**Nouns/Objects:** 
	The “things” we’ve been referring to in our state machines, for example.  
	Nouns have attributes and state.  
	They can be static, or dynamic.  
	Attributes of nouns can be public, or private.
**Verbs (Schell’s “Basic Action”):**  
	The basic actions the player can take.
**Actions (Schell’s “Strategic Action”):**
	The actions the player can take in the big picture.

# Player Promises
The principles of your creative design

Promises can be simple, or complex, but should be considered in every part of the tetrad.
Promises hold the designer accountable  
Promises provide the team a unified vision  
Promises guide us in decision making

3-5 promises being made from the designer to the player.
Often a short phrase or paragraph  
You keep these core principles in mind as you make decisions about everything in your game.  
Does this help deliver on your promise?  
Not just what your games has, but what your game is.


The term “Pillar” is common, typically shorter phrases you build the game off of.

Example:
**Subnautica Pillars**  
	<u>Intoxicating Creation: </u>The overwhelming excitement of being able to build anything.  
	<u>Thrill of the Unknown:</u> Excitement, dread and tension of exploring the unknown. No idea what  
	dangers/rewards are down there.  
	<u>Cascading Hysteria:</u> Uncontrollable outburst of emotion, fear, irrationality, laughter, weeping, etc. FTL style chain of "oh sh!t" dependencies, where a failure in one system can affect others, until you're suddenly in trouble.  

**Subnautica Values**  
	<u>Never tell the player what to do</u>. Let them decide. If there's something we want them to do, breadcrumb it.  
	<u>The world doesn't care about the player.</u> The player is a foreigner in an unknowable, majestic alien world. This lead to Non-Violence to creatures and the world.  
	<u>Respect the player and their time.</u> Don't manipulate them through achievements, dopamine and extrinsic rewards. Give them an amazing, rewarding experience that takes effort.
