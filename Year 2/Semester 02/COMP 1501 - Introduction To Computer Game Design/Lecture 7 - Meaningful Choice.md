### Flow
Game Flow often refers to the order of interactions for a game.

### Flow State
Term used to describe a state of productivity and is involved in the research of boredom and anxiety. You ignore other concerns, lose track of time, engage purely for the sake of engaging.

Flow states are mainly concerned with two factors:  
- Level of difficulty of the task at hand  
- Skill of the player engaging in the task

# Meaningful Choice

1. The player has more than one available course of action  
2. The player is only permitted to take fewer actions than the total number of options

Are we presenting a clear set of options? **Explicit**.  
Does the player need to discover options? **Implicit**.

---
# False Choice

An easy trap for game designers is introducing choice and thinking it’s an inherent improvement.  

1. Too many choices with little desire is unnecessarily overwhelming - choice paralysis  
2. Too few choices when the player has lots of desires is frustrating  
3. Sometimes a choice for one aspect removes choice for another!  
4. Sometimes one choice is clearly superior

# Stochastic vs Deterministic

In a deterministic system, the values of the current state completely determine the values of the next state.  

In a stochastic system, the values of the current state cannot determine the values of the next state.

More randomness tends to mean more casual play.  
More deterministic tends to mean more strategic play.
