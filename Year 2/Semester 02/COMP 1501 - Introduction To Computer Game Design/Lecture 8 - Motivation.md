### Extrinsic Motivation
Offering a reward for performing an action makes you more likely to perform that action.
Penalizing an action makes you less likely to do an action.

### Intrinsic Motivation
We as human beings are largely motivated by CAR(S)  
1. Competence  
	Competence means overcoming obstacles.
2. Autonomy  
	You make your own choices, you can act without so much fear of consequence.
3. Relationships  
	We crave shared experience, showing off, and nurturing.
4. (S)ensation


**Failing Competency**  
	You don’t know what you’re being asked to do  
	You’re not sure why you failed  
	You have no clue how to improve  
	The difficulty ramped up too quickly  
**Failing Autonomy**  
	The player doesn’t know what options are available  
	The player is paralyzed by choice  
	The choice clearly affects nothing  
	Choosing A is optimal, but B is what they really want

# MDA : Aesthetics

Aesthetics are the emotional result of the designed elements, and cannot be designed directly.

1. Sensation: Games as Sense-Pleasure  
2. Fantasy: Games as Make-Believe  
3. Narrative: Games as Drama  
4. Challenge: Games as Obstacle Course  
5. Fellowship: Games as Social Framework  
6. Discovery: Games as Uncharted Territory  
7. Expression: Games as Self-Discovery  
8. Submission: Games as Pastime

# Player Type Models

### Three Models of Motivation
1. Who are the players?
	Break the players into one of four categories to make broad generalizations about your demographics  
	Able to classify dynamics according to who would be interested

2. What are they playing?
	Examine the game from creator vs player perspective  
	Examine the harmony between creator intention and player experience  
	Classify creator/player experience outcomes in common terms

3. What's fun about it?
	An empirical breakdown of players and the games they play  
	Ranked motivations for players across a broad spectrum  
	Digging deeply into player specifics
