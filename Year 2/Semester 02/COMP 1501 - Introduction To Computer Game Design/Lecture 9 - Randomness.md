# Perception of Probability

Designers often lie about probability, or build systems to minimize its damage. 

**Pity Systems :**  
	• Only allow bad RNG n times  
	• “Smart” RNG:  
	• Skews randomness toward player needs  
	• Perceptive Skewing:  
	• Modify probability based on typical player perceptions of randomness.


#### Ways to Get Random Numbers  
**Uniformly random value**  
	e.g., rolling a die  
**Non-uniform random value**  
	E.g., rolling 2 dice has a higher probability of a 10 or 11 than a 3 or 18  
**Increasing certainty**  
	e.g., drawing cards from a deck without replacement / re-shuffling

# Input vs Output Randomness

#### Output Randomness
Take Action -> Random Output

Output Randomness typically exists in order to...  
	Abstract reality (accuracy, variable damage)  
	Promote the unknown/surprise (rewards, unexpected actions)  
	Stumble strategies (imperfect information, adapt from failure)


#### Input Randomness
Act after Luck

Input Randomness is about asking the player a question, and letting them answer.  
We generate a random state and the player can act within those constraints.


#### Summary
Input randomness limits actions, but doesn’t affect results.  
Output randomness offers action freedom, but ultimately determines the result.  
Modern design emphasizes positive rewards for post-luck.
