
A game is a problem-solving activity, approached with a playful attitude.

Generally we end up with, games:  
1. Entered willfully  
2. Have goals  
3. Have conflict  
4. Have rules  
5. Can be won and lost  
6. Are interactive  
7. Have challenge  
8. Create their own internal value  
9. Engage players  
10. Are closed, formal systems




---
## MDA (Mechanics, Dynamics, Aesthetics)

**Mechanics**
	The data, the algorithms. If statements, and answered questions. Specific, individual components of the game.

**Dynamics**
	The behaviour over time. How do the mechanics interact with each other? What kind of experience do these rules promote/dissuade?

**Aesthetics**
	The emotional response of the player. Why are they playing this game? What motivates them to play?

#### The Elemental Tetrad
**The Basic Elements of the Tetrad:**  
1. Mechanics: The procedures and rules of your game; mechanics are what makes a game a game.  
2. Story: The sequence of events unfolding in the game. Mechanics support the story.  
3. *Aesthetics: How the game looks, sounds, and feels. Aesthetics enhance and reinforce the story and mechanics.
